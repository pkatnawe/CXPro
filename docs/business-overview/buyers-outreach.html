Who to call & in what order

The prioritization, in one line

Texas is the speed beachhead (hottest pipeline, weakest incumbents). Canada is the sovereign serial-commissioner play (telcos building many sites, data-residency edge). India is volume-rich but margin-uncertain — Phase 2 unless a founder has real network there. The champion buyer everywhere is the Cx provider (CxA firm); owner-side teams buy tools directly. Re-verify every contact before outreach.

1 · Texas
Speed beachhead. 140+ planned projects, weak incumbents, greenfield owners (Crusoe, Oracle). Warm channel: the TCBCA board.
2 · Canada
Sovereign serial-commissioners. Telcos building many sites (Bell, Telus) + named Cx partner Quasar. Data-residency edge over US incumbents.
3 · India
Volume, Phase 2. Huge pipeline (AdaniConneX, NTT, CtrlS) but lower budgets weaken the value prop — pursue on founder network or after a NA reference.
Sequence by winnability, not size: prove it where it's easiest to land, expand to where it's biggest.

India call list

Named targets — India

India is the highest-volume market of the three and policy-supercharged — but strategically different, so we frame it honestly as a Phase-2 / opportunistic market, not the beachhead (unless founder network says otherwise). India crossed ~1.7 GW in 2026, doubled capacity in 2025, and targets 4.8 GW by 2030; the Union Budget 2026–27 grants a tax holiday to 2047 for foreign cloud providers hosting from India.1

The honest caveat: our per-MW pricing is anchored on US/Canada commissioning budgets ($200–360k/MW); Indian build costs, Cx budgets, and labor rates are lower, and cheaper local labor weakens the "AI multiplies scarce, expensive specialists" value prop that sells in North America. So India is volume-rich but margin-uncertain — pursue it if (a) a founder has real network/credibility there to shortcut cold enterprise sales, or (b) after a North-American reference proves the product. Geography is concentrated: Mumbai/Navi Mumbai (~47–52%) dominates, then Chennai (~15%), Hyderabad (~11%); Delhi-NCR (Noida) is a named, growing hub led by AdaniConneX. Commissioning is largely operator/GC-led — named third-party Cx firms are a discovery gap to fill before serious outreach.

The 90-day outreach sequence

Weeks 1–3 · Warm the TCBCA board

Join TCBCA, attend the next open virtual session, and have founder-to-founder conversations with the 5–6 named board members. Goal: not a pitch — a "show us your workflow, where does it hurt" discovery loop that also validates the willingness-to-pay question (see Pricing).

Weeks 2–6 · Chase the greenfield owners in parallel

Quasar (Bell AI Fabric — multi-site, named, no known tool), Bell's new in-house Cx hire, and Crusoe Abilene. These have active programs and no incumbent lock-in. Lead with the one demo that lands: cited procedure generation from their own uploaded spec.

Weeks 4–10 · Land one design partner

Convert the most engaged into a free pilot on one real test campaign. Success metric is the only one that matters: they use it on a live project and renew with money. One paying design partner de-risks more than a year of building.

Ongoing · Turn services firms into channels

Salute, SGS, HPD, Salas O'Brien run many projects with scarce engineers. A Cx provider who adopts CXPro brings it to every job they win — the org-scoped lesson engine makes that their compounding asset and our distribution.
