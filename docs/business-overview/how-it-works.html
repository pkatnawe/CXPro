How it works

One workflow, from design documents to a signed-off facility

Here's the full journey of a single piece of equipment — say, an air handling unit — through CXPro. Every step below happens in the product today, with AI accelerating the slow parts and humans approving anything that matters.

The end-to-end flow

From upload to invoice-ready handover

Upload the design package

The project team drags in specifications, submittal cut sheets, basis-of-design documents, and sequences of operations. No special formatting required — PDFs, drawings, and spreadsheets all work.

Who: Owner's Cx Agent or project admin · AI: classifies each document automatically

CXPro reads everything

In the background, AI classifies each document by type, breaks it into searchable passages, and extracts structured equipment data — manufacturer, model, capacities, setpoints — from cut sheets and schedules. Your project knowledge becomes queryable.

Who: fully automatic · typically complete within 24 hours

Register your assets

Equipment, systems, and spaces go into a structured registry — every asset tagged, located, and linked to the systems it belongs to. Import from a spreadsheet, an existing tool, or let AI extract the asset list from your equipment schedules.

Who: Cx team · AI: proposes the asset list from your documents

AI drafts the test procedures

Click "Generate Procedures" on any asset. The AI assembles the relevant spec passages, then drafts a step-by-step checklist or functional test — with every step citing the exact document passage it came from. If the AI isn't confident, it says so and tells you what's missing instead of guessing.

Who: AI drafts → Cx Engineer reviews · nothing becomes active without human acceptance

Review, accept, schedule

Engineers review drafts side-by-side with the cited sources, edit where needed, and accept. Accepted procedures become live test instances, scheduled against the commissioning levels — from pre-functional checklists (L2) through start-up (L3), functional performance tests (L4), and integrated systems testing (L5).

Who: Cx Engineer / OCA · every acceptance is recorded in the audit log

Execute in the field

Technicians work from tablets: today's assignments, clear pass/fail buttons, photo capture. A failed step automatically raises a deviation — no separate paperwork, nothing falls through the cracks.

Who: Field Technicians and Cx Engineers · works on site, optimized for tablets

Witness and sign

Formal witness sessions — where the owner's representative observes critical tests — are batched, scheduled, and digitally signed in the platform. Signatures attach directly to the test record.

Who: OCA, witnesses, and the executing engineer

Downstream paperwork takes care of itself

A signed witness session ripples through the system automatically: the test becomes eligible for the handover dossier, the linked billing milestone is earned, and the right people are notified — no one re-keys anything.

Who: fully automatic · powered by CXPro's internal event system

Hand over an audit-ready dossier

Turnover packages assemble per system or per project: every test, result, signature, deviation history, and the regulatory citations each test satisfies. What used to take weeks of binder-building becomes a review-and-export.

Who: OCA approves, Owner accepts · standards seeded include ASHRAE 202 and local building codes

The operating model

Where you live in the product

CXPro's interface is built around two surfaces that keep every role focused.

📥

Your Inbox — what needs you

The default landing view. Action items you own — a draft awaiting review, a failed test to disposition, an invite to accept — stay in your inbox until resolved. Pure awareness updates flow into a separate feed, so the to-do list is always real.

📊

Project Dashboard — how it's going

One click away: progress by discipline, open deviations by severity, the turnover pipeline, and compliance posture. Each role gets a dashboard preset shaped around its decisions.

Every detail page has AI built in. Open any asset, test, or deviation and an AI chat panel is already scoped to that exact record — ask "why did this step fail last time?" or "what does the spec say about this setpoint?" without explaining context.

Trust & security

Built for multi-company projects

Capital projects involve owners, general contractors, consultants, subcontractors, and inspectors — often competitors — working in one place. CXPro's security model is designed for exactly that.

🔐

Isolation at the database layer

Access control is enforced row-by-row in the database itself, keyed to each person's project participation — not just in the app. There is no code path that bypasses it.

👁️

Read widely, write your scope

By default, project members can see across disciplines (coordination needs visibility) but only change records in their own scope. Sensitive records can be confidentiality-scoped further.

📜

A complete audit trail

Every state change records who (or which AI agent), what, and when. AI actions are first-class citizens in the log — including the human confirmation that approved them.
