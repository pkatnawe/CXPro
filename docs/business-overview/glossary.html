Glossary of terms & abbreviations

Commissioning has its own language, and so does AI. This page defines every term and abbreviation used across these docs — in plain English, for readers from either world.

Commissioning fundamentals

The craft

Commissioning (Cx)
The structured process of verifying that a building's equipment and systems are designed, installed, and tested to perform as the owner intended — before the facility goes live.
Cx Levels (L1–L5)
The five stages of commissioning: L1 Factory Witness Testing (verifying equipment at the manufacturer's site) · L2 Pre-Functional Checklists (confirming correct installation) · L3 Start-Up (first energization and run) · L4 Functional Performance Testing (proving each unit performs to spec) · L5 Integrated Systems Testing (proving everything works together, including failure scenarios).
FPT
Functional Performance Test — the L4 test proving a piece of equipment meets its specified performance (flow rates, temperatures, response times).
IST
Integrated Systems Test — the L5 "final exam": whole-facility scenarios such as a simulated utility blackout, proving all systems respond together correctly.
Deviation
A formal record that something doesn't match design intent — a failed test step, an incorrect installation, an unexpected behavior. Tracked from discovery to resolution.
RFI
Request for Information — a formal question from the field to the design team ("the drawing shows X but the site has Y — which governs?"). Response time directly affects schedule.
Submittal
Vendor documentation (cut sheets, product data, shop drawings) submitted for approval to confirm proposed equipment matches the specification.
Punch list / Punch item
The list of remaining defects and incomplete items that must be closed before the owner accepts the work.
Change order
A formal, approved modification to the contracted scope, cost, or schedule — including equipment substitutions that affect testing.
Witness session
A formal event where the owner's representative observes critical tests being performed and signs off on the results.
Turnover package / Dossier
The complete, organized handover record for a system or project: test results, signatures, deviations, warranties, training records, and compliance evidence.
Substantial Completion
The contractual milestone at which the facility is usable for its intended purpose — typically triggering payment milestones and the warranty clock.
LOTO
Lock-Out / Tag-Out — the safety procedure for de-energizing and securing equipment before anyone works on it.
AHJ
Authority Having Jurisdiction — the inspector or regulatory body (fire marshal, building department) whose approval is legally required.

Roles

Who's who on a commissioning team

OCA
Owner's Commissioning Agent — the owner's program-level representative. Approves turnover packages, monitors compliance posture, and can override gates with documented justification.
CM
Construction Manager — the project-level orchestrator: coordinates disciplines and crews, schedules witness sessions, plans downtime windows, manages RFIs. A Cx provider's "Commissioning Manager" holds this same role — the job is identical; only the employer differs.
Cx Engineer / Specialist
The discipline-scoped engineer who authors and executes test procedures, marks results, and raises deviations.
Field Technician
The hands-on executor of assigned tests on site — often a subcontractor employee working from a tablet or phone.
GC
General Contractor — the company responsible for overall construction (e.g., DPR, Holder, PCL, Urbacon in our launch markets).
Discipline
A technical domain of work — Mechanical, Electrical, Controls, or General Construction. CXPro scopes teams, permissions, and milestones per discipline.
FM
Facility Manager — the downstream operator who runs the building after handover and inherits the commissioning record.

Documents & design

The paper trail

OPR
Owner's Project Requirements — the owner's statement of what the facility must achieve. The yardstick commissioning measures against.
BOD
Basis of Design — the design team's explanation of how the design meets the OPR: assumptions, calculations, and selected systems.
SOO
Sequence of Operations — the written logic describing how automated systems behave ("on loss of power, generator starts within 10 seconds, then…"). The source of truth for functional testing.
Cut sheet
A vendor's product datasheet: model numbers, capacities, electrical characteristics, dimensions.
As-built
Documentation updated to reflect what was actually constructed, as opposed to what was originally designed.
BIM
Building Information Modeling — the 3D digital model of the facility carrying both geometry and metadata (what each component is, its specs, and what it connects to).
Digital twin
A live digital replica of the physical facility, combining the model with real operational data.

Systems & infrastructure

The machinery

BMS
Building Management System — the central control system that monitors and automates HVAC, cooling, and other building systems through thousands of control points.
EPMS
Electrical Power Monitoring System — the system that tracks electrical distribution: breaker states, power quality, and events, often timestamped to the millisecond.
AHU
Air Handling Unit — the workhorse HVAC equipment that conditions and circulates air. Data centers have many; they appear throughout our examples.
Load bank test
Testing generators or power systems under artificial electrical load to prove rated capacity before the real IT load arrives.
Free cooling
Cooling using cold outdoor air or water instead of mechanical refrigeration — central to Canadian data center design, and complex to commission across the changeover.
ERCOT
Electric Reliability Council of Texas — the Texas grid operator. Its fast-frequency requirements and instability events shape how Texas facilities must prove power resilience.
AESO / Hydro-Québec / BC Hydro
Canadian grid operators and utilities (Alberta Electric System Operator, Québec, British Columbia) — the interconnection counterparties for Canadian builds.
Hyperscaler
A company operating data centers at massive scale — Meta, Microsoft, AWS, Google. The primary owners CXPro serves.
Colocation (colo)
A data center operator that leases capacity to multiple customers (Equinix, Digital Realty, eStruxture, QScale) — a key downstream recipient of commissioning data.
BACnet / Modbus / OPC UA
The dominant open communication protocols building and industrial equipment speak. Integrating with them is how software reads live data from chillers, generators, and controllers.
Niagara Framework
A widely-deployed vendor-neutral platform (by Tridium) that bridges 20+ building protocols — a single integration point through which software can reach most facility equipment.
Reality capture
Using cameras, 360° photos, or laser scanning (LiDAR) to record actual site conditions — which AI can then compare against the BIM model to detect installation deviations and track progress.

Standards & compliance

The rulebook

ASHRAE 202
The industry-standard commissioning process for buildings and systems — the framework most commissioning programs are contractually held to.
NFPA 72
The National Fire Alarm and Signaling Code — governs fire alarm system testing and acceptance.
OBC
Ontario Building Code — the provincial building regulation relevant to our Canadian market.
LEED
Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design — the green-building certification; commissioning is a prerequisite credit.
CQV (IQ/OQ/PQ)
Commissioning, Qualification & Validation — pharma's regulated equivalent of commissioning: Installation, Operational, and Performance Qualification. A future CXPro market.
Compliance mapping
CXPro's link between an individual test step and the regulatory citation it satisfies — turning compliance posture into a live dashboard.
Audit log
The tamper-evident record of every state change in CXPro: who (human or AI), what, and when. The backbone of regulator and owner trust.
COBie
Construction-Operations Building information exchange — the international standard format for handing structured facility data (equipment, warranties, maintenance info) from construction to operations. Commissioning agents have a formally defined role in producing it.
IFC
Industry Foundation Classes — the open, vendor-neutral file standard for exchanging BIM models between different software tools.
ASHRAE 223P
The 2024-published standard for machine-readable semantic tagging of building-system data — the convergence of the earlier Project Haystack and Brick Schema efforts. Its scope explicitly includes commissioning and digital twins.
Project Haystack / Brick Schema
The two pioneering semantic-tagging vocabularies for building data (e.g., labeling a sensor as "chilled water supply temperature" in a machine-readable way). Now converging under ASHRAE 223P.

AI terms

The AI vocabulary, demystified

AI agent
An AI system that doesn't just answer questions but performs multi-step work — retrieving documents, checking its own confidence, drafting records. In CXPro, agents are role-specific and always stop for human confirmation.
Grounding
Forcing the AI to base its output on retrieved source material (your specs and records) rather than its general training. The antidote to making things up.
Citation
A traceable link from an AI output back to the exact source passage it came from. In CXPro, citations deep-link to the page of the original document.
Hallucination
When an AI confidently states something false. CXPro's defenses: mandatory grounding, mandatory citations, confidence gating, and human confirmation.
Confidence gating / Handoff
The AI measures how well the sources support its draft; below a threshold it stops, names exactly what's missing, and hands the gap to the accountable person to resolve — then resumes. The opposite of both guessing and dead-ending. (Distinct from escalation, which in CXPro means routing an item up the chain when it sits unactioned past its deadline.)
Human-in-the-loop
The operating rule that AI output only takes effect after a qualified person reviews and confirms it. Every CXPro AI action works this way.
Multimodal AI
AI that understands more than text — voice, photos, video, thermal imagery, sound. The foundation of CXPro's field-capture roadmap.
Semantic search
Search by meaning rather than keywords — "what's the chiller failure response?" finds the right SOO passage even if it never uses those words.
Embedding / Indexing
The behind-the-scenes process of converting documents into a mathematical form the AI can search by meaning. Why uploaded packages become instantly queryable.
MCP
Model Context Protocol — an open standard that lets external AI tools securely connect to a platform's data and actions. CXPro exposes one, making it integrable with anyone's AI.
LLM
Large Language Model — the underlying AI model class (CXPro currently runs on Google Gemini, swappable by design).
Eval / Quality gate
A curated test set every CXPro agent must pass before updates ship. If accuracy or citation quality regresses, the release is blocked.
ML
Machine Learning — AI that finds patterns in data rather than following hand-written rules. The basis of our vibration/acoustic analysis bets.
OCR
Optical Character Recognition — extracting text from images, e.g. reading an equipment nameplate from a photo.
AR
Augmented Reality — overlaying digital information (test steps, model data) onto a live view of the physical world through a phone, tablet, or headset.
API / REST API
Application Programming Interface — the standard way one software system talks to another. "REST" is the dominant style. When we say a platform "has an API," it means we can integrate with it programmatically.

Business & field shorthand

Everyday abbreviations

MW
Megawatt — how data centers are sized (by power capacity, not square footage). A "60 MW facility" can power roughly 50,000 homes' worth of IT load; hyperscale campuses run 100–500+ MW.
PPE
Personal Protective Equipment — hard hats, gloves, hearing protection. Why field technicians can't easily type, and why voice capture matters.
ROI
Return on Investment — what a dollar spent returns. Commissioning's documented ROI is among the best in the building lifecycle (see the LBNL study on the Overview page).
KPI
Key Performance Indicator — a tracked metric, like "% of tests complete by discipline" or "deviation aging."
HPC
High-Performance Computing — supercomputing and AI-training workloads. HPC-ready campuses (like QScale in Québec) are a growing slice of the data center market.
O&M
Operations & Maintenance — as in O&M manuals, the vendor documentation the operations team relies on after handover.
QA/QC
Quality Assurance / Quality Control — the contractor's own inspection work, which runs alongside (but is distinct from) commissioning verification.

Industry references

Organizations & platforms named in these docs

AEC
Architecture, Engineering & Construction — the umbrella term for the building industry (as in Autodesk's "AEC Data Model API").
ACC
Autodesk Construction Cloud — Autodesk's construction management platform, one of the two dominant project backbones (with Procore) on our target projects.
Primavera P6 / MS Project
The two dominant master-scheduling tools on large construction projects. The schedule of record usually lives in one of them.
ENR
Engineering News-Record — the industry publication whose "Top 400" ranks the largest US contractors; used as a market-coverage benchmark (e.g., "62% of ENR Top 400").
LBNL
Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory — a U.S. Department of Energy national lab; its 1,500-building commissioning study is the gold-standard evidence for commissioning ROI.
CII
Construction Industry Institute — an academic-industry research consortium at the University of Texas; the original source of much of the rework-cost research we cite.
FMI
A construction-industry research and consulting firm; with Autodesk, the source of the "miscommunication drives ~26% of rework" finding.
BCxA
Building Commissioning Association — the professional association for commissioning providers; publisher of the best-practice guides we reference.
