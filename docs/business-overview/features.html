Core features

Everything a commissioning program needs, in one system

CXPro covers the full surface — assets, tests, issues, documents, handover, and teams — with AI woven through each area rather than bolted on the side. Features marked Live ship today; Roadmap items are designed and sequenced.

Foundation

Asset Registry Live

The job: "tell me what's installed, where it is, and whether it's ready — without reconciling three spreadsheets." Equipment lists scattered across vendor schedules, contractor trackers, and someone's laptop are why readiness questions take days to answer. CXPro keeps one structured inventory of everything on the project — and everything else links back to it.

🏷️

Assets, tagged and typed

Every piece of equipment carries its tag, manufacturer, model, serial, vendor, and nameplate data — with a full lifecycle from installation through retirement and decommissioning.

🌳

Spaces & hierarchy

Buildings, floors, rooms — spaces nest naturally, and assets can nest inside parent equipment. Find anything by where it physically is.

🔁

Systems

Assets group into the systems they serve — a chilled water plant, a fire alarm loop. System readiness drives when integrated testing can begin.

In the product today: a dense, filterable asset table (by space, type, status), per-asset detail pages with commissioning progress at a glance, and full create/edit/retire workflows. Where it's headed: every asset becomes a multimodal logbook — photos, voice notes, video, and thermal captures file themselves onto the unit's timeline, and AI analytics compares each unit against its own baseline, its model fleet-wide, and the program's accumulated lessons. See the AI Strategy for the full picture.

The core workflow

Commissioning Execution Live

The job: "get through a compressed test schedule without carrying open issues into IST." Commissioning inherits every upstream delay, so the testing window always shrinks — and the practitioners' cardinal rule is that deficiencies surfaced at L2–L4 must be cleared before integrated systems testing. That demands knowing, at any moment, exactly what's tested, what failed, and what's still open. The five levels, managed as structured workflows instead of binders:

LevelWhat it isIn CXPro
L1Factory witness testingTracked per asset; vendor-site flow on the roadmap
L2Pre-functional checklistsAI-drafted from your submittals; executed in the field
L3Start-upUnlocked when an asset is energized; results captured per attempt
L4Functional performance testsAI-drafted from sequences of operations; witnessed and signed
L5Integrated systems testingSystem-level tests across interacting equipment
📋

Templates & instances

Procedures are authored once as templates — by AI or by hand — then instantiated per asset or system. Reuse compounds across your equipment fleet.

🔄

Retest history

Each test keeps every attempt. Fail, fix, retest — the full history stays attached, which is exactly what auditors and owners want to see.

✍️

Witness sessions & signatures

Formal witnessing is batched and scheduled; digital signatures bind the witness to the result. Signed results trigger downstream handover and billing events automatically.

Knowledge

Documents & Project Intelligence Live

The job: "stop late design changes and incomplete vendor documents from silently invalidating my tests." An untracked edit to a control narrative ripples into settings, scripts, and safety plans — and incomplete vendor docs are among the most common commissioning derailers. Upload the design package once; everything downstream draws from it, with revisions tracked so the ripple is visible.

🗃️

Typed document library

Specs, submittals, sequences, drawings, manuals — classified automatically, with revision history so tests are always traceable to the document version they were written against.

🔎

Semantic search

Ask in plain language; CXPro finds the relevant passages across the entire package — the same index that grounds every AI answer and draft.

📊

Extracted equipment data

Structured specs pulled from cut sheets and schedules become queryable fields, linked to the assets they describe.

Staying on top of it

Inbox, Notifications & Issues

The job: "make sure nothing assigned to me falls through the cracks — and don't bury me in noise to do it." On a multi-company project the failure mode isn't missing information, it's information without an owner: the deficiency mentioned in a meeting, the retest nobody scheduled.

📥

Actionable Inbox Live

CXPro splits "things you must act on" from "things you should know." Action items persist in your inbox until resolved; awareness updates flow into a feed. Your to-do list stays honest — the single biggest ergonomic win over legacy tools.

🚨

Deviations & RFIs Roadmap

Failed test steps raise deviations automatically; AI triages severity and routes them. RFIs, punch lists, and change orders follow the same structured workflow — designed, sequenced next.

The payoff

Handover & Compliance Roadmap

The job: "close out the project without the closeout taking longer than the testing." Industry data says finishing the loose ends takes four times longer than planned1, and handover binders are assembled in a sprint nobody budgeted for. The reason commissioning exists is proving the facility works as designed — that proof should compile itself as the work happens.

📦

Turnover packages

Per-system or per-project dossiers assemble themselves from signed tests, deviation histories, warranties, and training records — review and export instead of weeks of binder-building.

⚖️

Compliance mapping

Test steps map to the regulatory citations they satisfy — ASHRAE 202, NFPA 72, local building codes — so compliance posture is a live dashboard, not a year-end scramble.

🏛️

Inspections & acceptance

Authority inspections and formal owner acceptance are tracked as first-class events — and acceptance automatically triggers warranty and billing milestones.

People

Teams & Access Live

The job: "get every company on the project into one system without a procurement fight or a security incident." Cx teams are chronically understaffed, and coordination across owner, GC, consultants, and subs is where miscommunication — the single biggest rework driver, at ~26%2 — breeds. The fix is making it trivially easy and free to bring everyone in.

🏢

Multi-organization

Owners, GCs, consultants, subs, designers, and inspectors each belong to their own organization, participating in shared projects with the right role.

✉️

Invitations that just work

Invite by email with a role; invitees who don't have accounts yet are seated automatically the moment they sign up.

💺

Unlimited users

Pricing is per project, not per seat. Inviting the whole sub crew never costs extra — adoption shouldn't be a procurement negotiation.
