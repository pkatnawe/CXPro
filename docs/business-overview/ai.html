AI in CXPro

AI that shows its work — and knows exactly when to bring in a human

In compliance-sensitive industries, a confident wrong answer is worse than no answer. CXPro's AI is engineered around one contract: everything it produces is grounded in your documents and cited to its source — and when it hits the edge of what it can verify, it doesn't bluff and it doesn't dead-end. It hands the decision to the right person, with everything they need to make it fast.

The golden rule

AI drafts. Humans decide. Everything is logged.

CXPro treats AI as a first-class member of the project team — with a deliberately limited badge. AI agents can read project knowledge and prepare work, but they can never change project state on their own. Every AI proposal arrives as a clearly-marked draft with one-click confirm, and the human confirmation is recorded in the audit log alongside the AI's reasoning trail.

📄

Grounded

Before drafting anything consequential, the AI retrieves the relevant passages from your actual project documents. No grounding, no draft.

🔗

Cited

Every generated test step, summary, and recommendation carries citations that deep-link to the exact page of the source document. Click and verify — the trust loop is one click long.

✋

Confirmed

Drafts only become real when a qualified human accepts them. AI-proposed actions appear in the interface with distinct visual treatment, so you always know what came from the machine.

AI feature 1

Document intelligence — your project, indexed

The foundation of every other AI feature. When you upload a design package, a fully automated pipeline goes to work:

Classify

Each file is automatically identified — specification, submittal cut sheet, basis of design, sequence of operations, drawing, schedule — so it's handled the right way.

Read & index

Documents are split into passages and semantically indexed, with page coordinates preserved — that's what makes citations deep-link to the exact spot in the source PDF.

Extract

Typed extractors pull structured data out of cut sheets, schedules, and sequences: manufacturers, models, capacities, setpoints. Equipment data becomes fields you can query, not prose buried in a PDF.

Business impact: the question "what does the spec actually say about this unit?" goes from a 20-minute document hunt to an instant, cited answer.

AI feature 2

Procedure generation — the flagship

Writing pre-functional checklists and functional test procedures by hand is the slowest, most error-prone part of commissioning prep. CXPro's execution agent automates the drafting:

What it does

For any registered asset, the agent gathers the relevant extracted specs and document passages, checks that it has enough information, and generates a structured, step-by-step procedure — each step typed, ordered, and cited. The draft lands as a reviewable record, never as live work.

What it does when something's missing

If the source material can't support a reliable draft — say, the cut sheet for that unit was never uploaded — the agent doesn't gamble and doesn't stall. It converts the gap into motion: it identifies exactly what's missing, proposes the fix ("I'd need the equipment cut sheet — should I create an upload request?"), and routes that request to the person who can resolve it. When the document arrives, the draft picks up where it left off. A gap becomes a fifteen-second decision in someone's inbox, not a stalled workstream.

The Day-1 promise: within 24 hours of uploading your design package, CXPro surfaces its first proposal — e.g. "I drafted 12 pre-functional checklists for AHU-01 through AHU-12 from your submittal package. Review and accept?" One click seeds your test backlog.

AI feature 3

Your team, mirrored in AI — one dedicated agent per role, coordinating like the team does

Commissioning isn't just equipment — it's people, and people-reality changes daily: a tech calls in sick, site hours shift, a vendor slips a delivery, a witness can't make Thursday. So CXPro doesn't ship one generic chatbot. It ships a team of agents that mirrors your team: each role gets its own AI, fluent in that role's job and pain points, operating strictly within that person's permissions — while reading from the same global project so the agents can coordinate with each other the way the humans do.

🎯

Dedicated to your role

The CM's agent thinks in crews, sequences, and look-aheads — it understands where the project hurts and what the team needs to keep moving. The engineer's agent thinks in specs and test logic. The tech's agent thinks in today's assignments. Same project, different minds.

🌐

Tapped into the whole project

Every agent reads from the shared backbone — the same assets, schedule, deviations, and documents. Your agent never has a partial picture; it has your view of the full picture.

🚧

With hard boundaries

Each agent inherits exactly its human's permissions — no more. It can't see what you can't see or touch what you can't touch. Cross-role coordination happens through the shared system, where every handoff is visible and audit-logged.

What that looks like when the project changes — and it always does

Tuesday, 6:40 AM: the chiller vendor's startup engineer is delayed two days, and one of the electrical techs is out sick. Here's the same disruption, handled by four coordinating agents — each surfacing only the decision its human actually owns:

The CM's agent re-plans the sequence

It sees the vendor slip and the absence, checks what's actually blocked versus what can pull forward, and proposes a reshuffled look-ahead: "L3 start-up on Chillers 3–4 slips to Thursday; AHU functional tests can move up — crew B is qualified and free. Approve?" The CM makes one decision, not twenty phone calls.

The engineer's agent reshuffles the test queue

It re-orders the week's procedures to match the new sequence, flags that the moved-up AHU tests reference a submittal revision still in review, and queues that review to the top of the engineer's morning.

The techs' agents update their day

Crew B's technicians open their tablets to a re-planned assignment list with an AI-suggested walking order — no morning huddle needed to figure out what changed. The sick tech's open items are already reassigned, not orphaned.

The OCA's agent watches the milestone

It assesses whether the two-day slip threatens the substantial-completion gate — concludes it doesn't, notes it in the daily brief, and stays quiet. The OCA's attention isn't spent on a problem the team already absorbed.

The point of the choreography: one disruption used to mean an hour of calls and a stale schedule by noon. With a dedicated agent per role coordinating through the shared backbone, the same disruption becomes four small, well-framed decisions — each delivered to exactly the person whose call it is, with the context to make it in seconds. The ecosystem absorbs the chaos; the humans keep their judgment for what deserves it.

The quality contract

How CXPro guarantees AI quality

The riskier the AI's output, the stricter the rules it operates under — and the more deliberate its handoff to a human. This tiered contract is enforced in code, not in a policy document.

Graceful under failure, by design

Most AI products are impressive until something goes wrong. CXPro is engineered for the moment things go wrong — because on a commissioning project, they will. The pattern is always the same three moves:

📡

1. Detect

Every AI output carries a measured confidence score derived from how well the sources support it. The system knows the difference between "well-grounded draft" and "plausible guess" — before a human ever sees it.

🧭

2. Route

Below threshold, the work doesn't fail — it's handed off. The gap becomes an action item in the inbox of the one person whose role makes it theirs to resolve: the missing cut sheet goes to the document owner, the ambiguous spec conflict to the design engineer, the risky call to the OCA.

▶️

3. Resume

When the human supplies the missing piece or makes the call, the AI picks the work back up automatically. Nobody re-explains context, nobody restarts the task. The loop closes itself.

The payoff is focus, not just safety: the AI absorbs the routine work entirely, and when it hands work off, it hands it to the right role with the decision pre-framed. An OCA's attention goes to acceptance calls, an engineer's to technical judgment, a CM's to sequencing trade-offs — and nobody's day is spent on work the AI could have finished or questions meant for someone else.

🎚️

Visible confidence on every draft

Each AI draft displays a three-tier confidence rating (high / medium / low) derived from how well the source material supports it. Below "low," the work is handed off to the right human automatically rather than gambling.

🧪

Quality gates before every release

Every agent is measured against a curated test set before any update ships. If citation accuracy, handoff judgment (knowing when to bring in a human), or correctness regresses, the release is blocked.

👍

Your corrections make it smarter

Every AI output carries thumbs up/down and a "correct this" option. Corrections feed directly into the evaluation set and model tuning — the system measurably improves with use.

🧾

A full flight recorder

Every agent run is stored immutably: what it retrieved, what it produced, what it cited, who confirmed it. When a regulator or owner asks "where did this procedure come from?", the answer is one click away.

Under the hood (for the technically curious): CXPro's AI service runs on Google Gemini models orchestrated through typed, structured pipelines — citation requirements are encoded in the output schemas themselves, so the system literally cannot return an uncited draft. The architecture is provider-agnostic, so models can be upgraded or swapped as the state of the art moves.
