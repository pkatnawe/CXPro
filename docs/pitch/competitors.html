The honest read

The question isn't whether there's AI anymore — it's whether you can trust it

CxPlanner especially is real, fast, and aimed at the same data-center buyers. So we don't lead with "no one has AI." The more useful — and more defensible — point is what's missing from everyone's AI today, on a job where a wrong answer is signed off and lived with for years:

❓

None of them cite their sources

Competitors generate checklists, but we found no one who makes each generated step traceable to the exact clause in the spec or submittal it came from. On a sign-off-driven build, an answer you can't check is an answer you can't use.

🎰

None of them say "I'm not sure"

Their AI fills in templates and answers questions. We found no one whose AI refuses when the documents don't support an answer — the single most important behavior when the cost of a confident mistake is a failed system at handover.

Our wedge

We compete on trust, verifiability, and depth — not on "having AI"

🧾

Grounded & cited

Every drafted step links to the source document. The output is checkable, which is what makes it usable on a mission-critical build — and it's the gap no competitor publicly fills.

🛑

Refuses when unsure

It won't invent a procedure the documents don't support — it flags the gap instead. Trustworthy beats impressive when someone has to put their name on it.

🔎

Verifiable deviation detection

Catching a spec-vs-submittal conflict only matters if you can see why and trust it. We treat that as a checkable, traceable capability, not a slideshow demo.

🧑‍🔧

Agents per role, one objective

Not just task automation — an assistant for each seat, all working toward keeping the project on schedule and on spec. Coordination, not just generation.

🪜

Built for the hard case

The full mission-critical data-center workflow, through the final integrated systems test — the most demanding commissioning there is.

🔁

Improves with use

Each project sharpens it on your team's standards — a body of feedback a fast follower can't copy quickly.

The honest version

This isn't an empty field. CxPlanner is a real, fast-moving competitor going after the same data-center buyers with shipping AI, and Bluerithm is close behind. The generic "AI drafts a checklist" ground is already contested. Our bet is the part that's still open and that matters most where the stakes are highest: AI you can trust — grounded in the documents, cited, willing to say "I'm not sure," and built around the real, sign-off-driven workflow — plus out-executing on the things that earn an engineer's trust. We don't win by being the only one with AI. We win by being the one a commissioning engineer is willing to stake their signature on.
