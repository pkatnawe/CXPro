How we'd price it

Our current thinking, in plain terms — and the open questions we're still working through.

The idea

Charge a small slice of the value, on a number the buyer already uses

Today's tools charge per user — a few thousand dollars against a multi-million-dollar commissioning budget. We'd price against the value instead: a small, fixed amount per megawatt of the data center (the size unit buyers already plan and budget in).

⚡

Priced per megawatt

Bigger project, bigger fee — automatically, on a number that's known from the start and can't be fudged. Early deals are modest: a small ~20 MW build is around $100k; a 100 MW campus, a few hundred thousand; a gigawatt buildout, into the millions.

🔒

Predictable, fixed up front

One number, locked when you sign. No per-seat fees, no usage meter, no surprise charges. Roughly 1–2% of the commissioning budget — big value, still a rounding error to the buyer.

👥

Everyone's included

Unlimited users. We want every person on the project in the system, so we never charge by the seat.

It isn't only for data centers. The same idea works for other big facilities — we'd just swap the unit (per hospital bed, per clean-room area). So it's not a data-center dead-end.

Open questions

What we're still working through

The pricing model

  • Which model: per-megawatt value pricing, a subscription, usage, or an outcome share — which fits an enterprise AI buyer best right now? We deliberately avoided per-seat.
  • What's working in enterprise AI today: how comparable AI products are actually getting priced into large, regulated buyers — and what falls flat.
  • First customers: charge the first design partners (discounted) or give it away to win the logo — and how much.
  • The first real quote: no one else publishes a price like this, so we'd be setting the benchmark — how aggressive to be.
  • Land and expand: structuring the first deal so one site naturally grows into the whole portfolio.
