CXPro · AI for data-center commissioning

Commissioning is the last step before a data center makes money. We're the AI that gets you through it faster.

Before a data center can open, every power and cooling system has to be tested and signed off — that's commissioning. It's slow, manual, and done by a handful of hard-to-hire experts. CXPro is an AI that does the heavy lifting: it drafts the tests, catches the problems, and keeps the whole project on schedule.

What it is

An AI teammate for everyone on the project

CXPro reads your project's design documents and sets up the whole commissioning plan on day one. Then it works alongside the team — drafting, checking, and flagging — while a person makes every real decision.

🧑‍🔧

An assistant for every role

Each person — the lead, the engineer, the field tech — gets an AI that does the busywork for their job: drafting test procedures, prepping reports, and flagging what needs their attention today.

🛑

It catches problems instantly

The moment a test fails, CXPro raises the issue and routes it to the right person — so nothing piles up to the end, where it's most expensive to fix.

🧾

It always shows its work

Every answer points back to the document it came from, and it says "I'm not sure" instead of guessing. On a mission-critical build, that trust is everything.

🔁

It gets smarter as you use it

It learns your team's standards from quick, in-the-flow feedback — so it's useful on day one and better on every project after.

Why we can still win

AI is starting to arrive in commissioning — but not the kind you can trust on a sign-off

🧱

Most tools are still forms

Much of the category (CxAlloy, BlueRithm, Facility Grid) is digital checklists and issue logs — it records the work rather than doing it.

🎰

The new AI isn't grounded

A fast-moving competitor or two now generate checklists with AI — but we found none that cites its sources or refuses when it isn't sure. On a build someone has to sign off, that trust gap is the opening.

📈

The data compounds

Each project sharpens the system on your team's standards — a body of feedback a fast follower can't copy quickly.

Focus

The same system can commission anything — we're starting with data centers on purpose

Commissioning is the same discipline for hospitals, labs, pharma plants, and chip fabs — verify the build matches the design, test by levels, document, hand over. CXPro works for all of them. We're focused on data centers first because that's where the demand and spending are concentrated right now: AI clouds (FluidStack, Crusoe, CoreWeave) and hyperscalers are commissioning many large sites at once on tight deadlines and building their commissioning teams now, with no existing software in place — a clear first market, not the limit of the product.
